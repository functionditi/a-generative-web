Instructions #1

One

Take a piece of paper and fold each of the four sides in parallel to the edge. However, make sure you are not dividing the paper by half. Now you have formed a rectangle in the page when all the fold intersect.

Two

Next, take the top left corner of the page and fold it so that the top left corner of the rectangle is a point on the new fold. Repeat this 5 times for the top left corner.

Three

Repeat steps 2 for every other page corner and the corresponding rectangle corner.

Four

Yay, you’re done!

Outcomes

Instructions #2

One

Draw a curved line for five seconds. Mark the beginning and the end with a big dot.

Two

Make a second curved line that intersects your previous line twice. Mark the beginning and the end with a big dot.

Three

Repeat 10 times.

Outcomes

Instructions #3

One

Take the paper and draw a circle that covers more than 1/2 the paper surface area. Don’t worry about the square being perfect.

Two

Divide that circle into 25 rectangles. No two rectangles can be the same size.

Three

Fill in the smallest 5 rectangles (let’s call this set A) with 20 diagonal lines each.

Four

Fill in the next set of 5 rectangles (let’s call this set B, with rectangles bigger than set A) with 10 diagonal lines each.

Five

Fill in the next set of 5 rectangles ( (let’s call this set C, with rectangles bigger than set B) with 5 diagonal lines each.
